New Client Cheat Sheet

Everything you need to tell first-time visitors

What to Tell New Clients

Prep instructions for first-timers

  • 1
    "Come with a clean face" — no makeup, sunscreen, or moisturizer. We'll cleanse again, but starting fresh helps.
  • 2
    "Skip retinol and exfoliants" — for 3 days before facials or peels. Stop blood thinners (aspirin, fish oil) 24 hours before injectables.
  • 3
    "Eat before you come in" — especially for injectables. Low blood sugar can make clients lightheaded.
  • 4
    "Bring your medication list" — or just snap a photo of the bottles. We need to check for interactions.
  • 5
    "Write down your questions" — clients always forget when they're in the room. Remind them beforehand.
First Visit Flow

Walk them through the process

  • Check-in
    Hand them intake forms. Health history, allergies, current skincare routine. Takes about 10 minutes — offer water while they wait.
  • Consult
    Provider meets with them. Skin assessment, goals discussion, treatment plan. Remind nervous clients there's no pressure to decide today.
  • Treat
    Treatment time. Most services take 15-60 minutes. If they ask about pain — numbing cream is available for injectables.
  • Checkout
    Aftercare + rebooking. Give them the printed aftercare sheet and book their follow-up before they leave.

Remind callers: first-time consultations are complimentary. They don't have to commit to any treatment on the first visit.

Aftercare Basics

The quick version

After Botox/Fillers
Do: stay upright 4 hours, ice gently
Avoid: exercise, alcohol, hot showers for 24 hrs
After Facials
Do: moisturize, use SPF 30+
Avoid: makeup for 12 hours, exfoliants for 48 hrs
After Peels
Do: keep skin hydrated, let it peel naturally
Avoid: picking, sun exposure, retinol for 1 week
After Laser
Do: apply aloe, wear loose clothing over area
Avoid: sun, hot water, deodorant on treated area 48 hrs
