When Someone Calls About a DWI

What they need to know first

  • ✓
    Time is critical. They have only 15 days from arrest to request an Administrative License Revocation (ALR) hearing, or their license will be automatically suspended.
  • ✓
    Don't talk to police about the case without an attorney present. Anything they say can be used against them.
  • ✓
    Keep all paperwork from the arrest — citation, temporary license, bond papers, everything.
  • ✓
    Document everything they remember about the arrest while it's fresh in their memory.
Preparing for Their Consultation

What to bring and expect

  • 1
    All arrest paperwork — citation, bond papers, temporary license, any receipts from jail
  • 2
    Driver's license and insurance information
  • 3
    Timeline of events — what they remember happening before, during, and after the stop
  • 4
    Questions prepared — about the process, potential outcomes, and next steps

Remind them: The consultation is confidential. They should be completely honest about what happened — it helps the attorney build the best defense strategy.

Common Questions You'll Get

Quick answers for worried callers

Q: "Will I go to jail?"
A: That depends on many factors. Mr. Hull will review their specific case and explain all possible outcomes during the consultation.

Q: "Can I still drive?"
A: They should have received a temporary license good for 40 days. After that, it depends on whether they requested the ALR hearing.

Q: "How much will this cost?"
A: Legal fees vary by case complexity. Mr. Hull discusses fees transparently during the consultation — no hidden costs.

Q: "What if this is my second DWI?"
A: Second offenses have more serious consequences, but there are still defense options. It's even more important to act quickly.
