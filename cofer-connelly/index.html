Consultation Preparation

What clients should bring

Save time by letting clients know what to gather before they come in:

  • 1
    All paperwork — Citations, arrest records, court documents, protective orders
  • 2
    Timeline of events — Written summary of what happened and when
  • 3
    Contact info — Names and numbers for any witnesses
  • 4
    Financial records — For family cases: pay stubs, tax returns, bank statements
  • 5
    Photo ID & insurance card — For intake forms and records
Scheduling Smart

Triage by urgency

Court date within 7 days — Priority scheduling, same or next day if possible

Active warrant — Urgent, needs immediate attorney consultation

Emergency protective order — Time-sensitive, affects housing and children

Child custody dispute — Schedule within 48 hours if children's safety is involved

Pro tip: If someone calls crying or extremely panicked, get their callback number first — before gathering details. Sometimes they hang up and you've lost them.
